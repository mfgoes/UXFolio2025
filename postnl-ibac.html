The Challenge

PostNL's digital customer journey for business clients was cumbersome and inefficient.

  • 18 steps, 37 clicks in the original flow
  • Up to 14-day waiting periods before customers could start shipping
  • 4.7% conversion rate in 2023
  • Poor customer satisfaction scores

My Role

Transformed complex business onboarding through user research, prototype validation, and cross-team collaboration.

  • Copy optimization: Designed and tested interactive prototypes with target users throughout design iterations
  • Plugin setup flow: Data-driven design decisions through A/B testing and conversion optimization
  • Usability testing: Led user research sessions to understand customer archetypes/segment
  • Progressive disclosure: Applied design thinking methodology to validate assumptions before development, aiding the initiation of a data driven culture at PostNL.

Major Features & Optimizations

Key product improvements spanning multi-product selection, A/B testing wins, onboarding integration, AI implementation, and user research insights.

Strategic Journey Segmentation (Q2 2024)

Recognizing that different customer segments have distinct needs, we introduced a bifurcated onboarding approach that separates high-volume shippers (10k+ packages annually) from standard digital customers. High-volume prospects now receive immediate personal outreach rather than being funneled through the standard form-based process. Results: 42 total qualified leads generated through targeted segmentation €517,005 revenue won from high-volume segment alone Improved resource allocation by directing enterprise customers to sales while maintaining digital self-service for smaller businesses Enhanced touchpoint relevance matching customer complexity with appropriate service level

Strategic Journey Segmentation (Q2 2024)

Reducing Friction Through Step Elimination Completely redesigned the account creation process, reducing required steps from 18 to 9 while maintaining all essential verification and setup requirements. Combined related actions into logical groupings and eliminated redundant data entry points that were causing customer abandonment.
Results: 50% step reduction from 18 to 9 required steps Reduced cognitive load through logical step grouping and progressive disclosure Eliminated redundant verification and duplicate data entry points Contributed to overall IBAC improvements including 94.5% activation rates (vs 77%) and 22.6% faster time-to-shipment

Confirmation Screen & Preference Capture (Q4 2024)

Introduced a dedicated confirmation screen as the final step in the IBAC onboarding flow, transforming what was previously an abrupt process end into an engaging completion experience. The screen captures critical customer preferences including expected start date and communication preferences, enabling personalized follow-up sequences and improved customer success. Results: 85% preference completion rate - customers actively provide start date and communication preferences 12% faster time-to-first-shipment for customers who indicate "ready to start immediately" 15% higher email engagement through targeted newsletter and tips based on captured preferences Improved customer satisfaction with clearer expectations and personalized communication flow

Research Methods Used

A/B Testing & Conversion Optimization

Systematic testing of page layouts, content hierarchy, and interaction patterns. Also analyzed competitor flows from DHL and DPD for baseline insights and differentiation opportunities.

In-Field Research (Klantsafari)

Visited clients in their natural environments - offices and warehouses - to understand real PostNL software usage and onboarding pain points. This revealed the gap between designed experience and actual user behavior.

Prototype Testing

Developed and tested clickable prototypes with target users throughout design iterations.

Conclusion

The IBAC project showcased the impact of systematic UX transformation on business outcomes. Starting with a cumbersome 18-step process that frustrated customers, we used research insights and iterative testing to create a streamlined experience that nearly doubled conversion rates. The work required balancing competing priorities—from technical constraints across multiple development teams to business pressures for quick wins. Success came from maintaining focus on user needs while building stakeholder buy-in through data and incremental improvements. The €500K+ revenue impact and external recognition validated this approach, but more importantly, we created a foundation for continued optimization and customer success.
